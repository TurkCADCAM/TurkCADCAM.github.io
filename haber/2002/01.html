Explosive Structuring for High-Speed Generation of Mold Surface Structures:

Plastic parts often need a structured surface for decoration and for lamination. These structures usual were stamped as a negative in the appropriate production mold. Natural surface textures, such as leather grains or leaves, are currently engraved or etched into injection molding tools by highly time-consuming or costly techniques. Such conventionally generated structures must not be of a too regular and thus artificial appearance, a demand that calls for high input in design and shaping. New solutions of high economy, therefore, are desirable by which to obtain surface structures of true-to-detail appearance.

Explosive structuring enables the direct stamping of surface textures into metals. By means of a detonative shock wave a given structure can be transferred to a metal surface in a split second. An object with the desired surface structure is placed onto the metal surface. The structuring object is then blasted with a thin explosive layer, caused by this detonation the structure is exactly stamped into the surface of the metal.
